Findings:¶

  • The average budget for each movie in this dataset was found to be 1.462570e+7 dollars and standard deviation 3.091321e+7 which means the data for budget is not so spread out. Values present in the revenue column range from 0 - 2.781506e+9 dollars which will be checked in the cleaning phase to investigate reasons for this.

Conclusions¶

From exploring the data I found that:

  • From the data provided, the top 10 movies were not part of the top 10 movies based on revenue.
  • All movies except "Avatar" and "Avengers: Age of Ultron" did not have budgets close to the ones in the top 10 movie list based on budget.
  • 70% of top 10 movies movies based on revenue had average vote of 7.0 and above.
  • Most movies (70%) in the top 10 categroy based on average vote where from the 70s, 80s, 90s.

We can't clearly conclude that putting in money to make a movie results in great revenue because if we went deeper to see the individual movies in a particular year we get more information on the highest and lowest revenues in that particular year. Humans are complex so other variable like genre can contribute to the result gotten here, economic climates in different countries and years could contribute to either increasing the budget or reducing the revenue.

I can only say that from the data set provided for this project, great budget allocations don't translate into great revenue as profit.

Limitation¶

There are more than 5 - 10% of data having null values, erroneus or missing values. These might lead to wrong predictions or biased analysis.
